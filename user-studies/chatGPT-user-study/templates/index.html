Project Title: Explaining Semantic Divergences to Bilingual Speakers (IRB Package Number 2018458-1)
About the study: The purpose of this study is to investigate ways to improve human and machine collaboration in detecting meaning differences between texts written in different languages using explainable automated reasoning.
  • The study takes up to 30 minutes to complete.
Data and Confidentiality: The study collects minimal demographic information, such as age and gender. You can opt-out of answering demographic questions. Crowdworker ID is the only potentially identifying information, which will be immediately removed from the data after participants are paid. All data will be anonymized prior to public release and responses cannot be linked to individuals. Any potential loss of confidentiality will be minimized by storing data in a secured password protected database.
Right to Withdraw and Questions: Participation in the study is completely voluntary. If you decide to participate in this research, you may stop participating at any time. If you have questions, concerns, or complaints, or if you need to report an injury related to the research, please contact the investigator:

Risks: There are no known risks associated with participants of this study. You will be able to skip questions you do not want to answer or end you participation at any time.
Benefits: There are no direct benefits for participants. This study will make it possible to encourage bilingual speaker’s sensitivity in detecting nuanced differences in meaning across languages, via automatic annotations grounded in specific text spans. We hope that in the future, other people might benefit from this study by gaining an understanding of differences between languages and cultures, based on evidence from publicly available text data.
